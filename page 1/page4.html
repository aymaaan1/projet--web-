Atlantic Coast, Morocco

Mogador · Essaouira

Where the Atlantic wind sculpts ramparts of ivory stone — a blue city dreaming at the edge of the sea.

Overview

The Wind City of the South

Essaouira — the ancient Mogador — is Morocco’s most romantic port. Its medina, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, unfolds in a maze of whitewashed walls, indigo shutters, and sea-salt air. The ramparts overlook the crashing Atlantic, guarded by Portuguese cannons pointing into the haze.

Beyond the medina walls, pristine beaches stretch south for kilometres, whipped by the trade winds that make Essaouira the kitesurfing capital of Africa. Inland, argan orchards silver the hillsides, tended by Berber cooperatives for centuries.

Our journeys blend the city’s Gnaoua soul, its artisan craft workshops, its seafront fish grills, and the wild Atlantic coast into a seamless experience of culture and nature.

Experiences

Moments Carved by the Wind

Ramparts at Sunset

Walk the seventeenth-century fortifications as the Atlantic blazes orange. The cannon-lined Skala is one of the most cinematic viewpoints in all of Morocco.

Medina Craft Trail

Marquetry workshops, thuya wood carvers, silversmith souks — Essaouira’s artisans are among Morocco’s finest. Your guide opens hidden ateliers to visitors.

Gnaoua Music Night

The spiritual home of Gnaoua music — the city that hosts Africa’s greatest world music festival every June. Hear the guembri and qraqeb by firelight in a private riad courtyard.

Kitesurfing & Windsurf

The Alizée wind that earned Essaouira the name “Wind City” makes it a world-class kitesurfing destination. Lessons for all levels on the long southern beach.

Argan Forest Excursion

Journey inland through silver-green argan orchards. Visit a Berber women’s cooperative and taste fresh argan oil pressed the traditional way — a product native to no other place on earth.

Harbour & Fish Grill

The blue boats of Essaouira’s fishing fleet bring in their catch at dawn. Choose your fish from the harbour stalls and have it grilled before you on the port terrace.

Sample Itinerary

Two Days on the Atlantic Shore

Day one — Arrival

Marrakech to the Blue City

Depart Marrakech by private transfer along the road through Chichaoua and the argan forests. Arrive by midday, settle into your medina riad. Afternoon guided walk through the souks, Mellah, and artisan quarter. Sunset on the Skala ramparts, followed by dinner at a sea-view restaurant.

Day two — Immersion

Wind, Sea & Soul

Morning visit to the fishing harbour and fresh breakfast at a port café. Choose your experience: kitesurfing lesson on the beach, argan forest excursion, or a thuya wood carving workshop. Evening: Gnaoua music in a riad courtyard with dinner and mint tea.

Day three — Optional extension

Coast & Return

Explore the wild coastline south towards Diabat — the village of Jimi Hendrix legend — and the ruins of Borj El Berod rising from the sand dunes at the river mouth. Late afternoon return to Marrakech or onward to your next destination.
